Space Facts

Here are some facts about space:

  • There are 8 planets in our solar system.

  • There are 5 dwarf planets in our solar system.

    • The 5 dwarf planets are Ceres, Pluto, Eris, Haumea, and Makemake.

  • Jupiter, the fifth largest planet from the Sun, is more than twice the size of all planets within out solar system combined.

  • The largest object in the universe is Hercules-Corona Borealis Great Wall, which is a galaxy cluster.

  • The universe is around 13.8 billion years old.

  • The largest volcano in our solar system is Olympus Mons on Mars. It is 16 miles high. That's roughly three times larger than Mount Everest!

  • The Sun's Mass takes up around 99.86 percent of our solar system.

  • The sunset on Mars appears blue. This is due to the fact that the atmosphere on Mars is very thin.

  • Halley's Comet will not pass Earth again until 2061.

  • When NASA was first designing space suits, they cost rougly $12,000,000.

    • To add to this, the cost of a space suit in today's money would be around $150,000,000.

  • It will take 3.75 billion years for the Andromeda and Milky Way galaxies to collide.

  • Saturn has the most moons in our solar system, with 82 that have been discovered.

  • Uranus has a hexagonal-shaped storm that is 10 times larger than Earth.

  • One Earth season is the equivalent to 40 years on Earth.

  • There is a disk that surrounds each black hole, and it is called an accretion disk.

  • Gene Cernan was known for writing his daughter's intials on the moon.

    • He was also the last person to walk on the moon.

  • The coldest section of the universe is the Boomerang Nebula.

    • It is so cold that it is -457 degrees Fahrenheit.

  • The types of galaxies in the universe are spiral, elliptical, and irregular.

    • Of these, the most common are elliptical galaxies.

  • The nearest star to Earth is Proxima Centauri, which is 4.2 light years away.

  • The types of stars in the universe are red dwarfs, red giants, white dwarfs, and blue giants.

    • Of these stars, red dwarf stars are the smallest. The largest are red giants.

  • The largest galaxy in the universe is the Andromeda galaxy.

  • Not many people talk about it, but the third astronaut to walk on the moon was Pete Conrad.
